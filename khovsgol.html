Lake Khövsgöl

Mongolia’s Blue Pearl – Pristine waters and untouched wilderness

About Lake Khövsgöl

Lake Khövsgöl, often called the "Blue Pearl of Mongolia," is one of the largest and deepest freshwater lakes in Asia. Located in the northern part of the country near the Russian border, it is surrounded by mountains, forests, and rich biodiversity.

Things to Do

  • Take a boat ride or kayak on the crystal-clear lake
  • Hike in the surrounding mountains and forests
  • Experience Tsaatan reindeer herder culture
  • Visit local nomadic families and explore the national park
  • Enjoy fishing, stargazing, and serene nature

Wildlife and Nature

The region is home to ibex, moose, bears, wolves, and many bird species. The lake itself holds about 70% of Mongolia’s fresh water and is incredibly clean and cold year-round.

How to Get There

You can fly or drive from Ulaanbaatar to Mörön, the nearest city. From Mörön, it's about a 100 km drive to the lake. Many guided tours offer transportation and lodging.
